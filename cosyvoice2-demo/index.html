Research Overview

Challenge

Adapting state-of-the-art TTS models like CosyVoice2 to European languages (French and German) while maintaining quality and naturalness requires understanding which components benefit most from fine-tuning.

Methodology

Systematic investigation of component-wise fine-tuning strategies: SLM, Flow matching, and HiFiGAN vocoder, analyzing their individual and combined impact on French and German speech quality.

Innovation

First comprehensive analysis of fine-tuning efficiency in modern TTS pipelines, providing insights into optimal training strategies for cross-lingual adaptation.

Key Findings

Most Impactful Component

Flow Matching fine-tuning provides the largest improvement in naturalness and prosody, with MOS scores increasing by 0.8 points.

Data Efficiency

Significant improvements achieved with just 2-4 hours of French training data when fine-tuning all components.

Component Synergy

Combined fine-tuning of all three components yields synergistic effects, outperforming individual component fine-tuning.
